My First Days at University

Nervousness in a New Environment

When I first arrived at university, everything around me felt completely new and unfamiliar. The campus was large, the people were strangers, and the system was very different from what I was used to. I felt nervous attending classes, speaking in front of others, and even starting simple conversations. This nervousness affected my confidence, and at times I doubted myself. However, as days passed, I slowly started understanding the environment and became more comfortable.

Emotional Struggle of Being Away from Home

Being away from home was one of the most difficult challenges I faced. I missed my family, daily routines, and the emotional support that I always had. There were moments when I felt lonely, especially during quiet times or after a long day. Adjusting to a new place without family was not easy, but it taught me how to manage my emotions and become more independent. Over time, I learned to handle this distance and grow stronger mentally.

Sacrifice for a Better Future

Coming to university was not just a step forward, but also a big sacrifice. Leaving my comfort zone, facing difficulties, and staying away from loved ones were all part of this journey. Despite the hard conditions, I stayed focused on my goal of building a successful future. These sacrifices motivated me to work harder and not give up. I realized that achieving something meaningful in life always requires patience, dedication, and the courage to face challenges.
